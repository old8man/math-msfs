\begin{abstract}
\noindent
The proof assistant \Verum{} is analysed as a concrete computable instance of the $(\infty, n)$-categorical moduli space $\fM$ of Rich-foundations classified by the MSFS programme. Four structural correspondences are established: (i) the \texttt{@framework(name, citation)} axiom mechanism realises the stratum $\LFnd$ of Rich-foundation points with explicit bibliographic coordinate; (ii) \texttt{protocol}-based abstraction instantiates the classifier stratum $\LCls$ with explicit (M1)--(M5) data; (iii) the LCF-style trusted kernel (\texttt{verum\_kernel}, target $<$ 5\,000 LOC) embodies the T-2f* depth-stratification axiom and inherits universal paradox-immunity via the Yanofsky-reducibility theorem; (iv) the nine-strategy \texttt{@verify(...)} dispatch ladder refines the $\nu$-invariant of Diakrisis in an operational-cost gradient. As empirical validation the 223-theorem UHM proof corpus stratifies exactly into MSFS levels. Beyond integration, the paper argues \Verum{} occupies a unique position no extant proof assistant can match --- \Coq, \Lean, \Agda, \Isabelle, \Fstar, Dafny, Liquid Haskell, Mizar, Metamath each violate at least one MSFS-level invariant which \Verum{} satisfies structurally. The gap is not engineering debt but methodological: \Verum{} was designed after MSFS, under MSFS.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Proof assistants have accumulated for six decades. Each embodies a specific choice of foundation: \Coq{} / \textsc{Rocq} realise the Calculus of Inductive Constructions with universe polymorphism; \Lean{} 4 realises a dependent type theory with proof irrelevance and classical additions; \Agda{} ships cubical type theory natively; \Isabelle{} / HOL implements a weakly classical higher-order logic; \Fstar{} couples refinement types with effects; Dafny targets SMT-discharged contracts over an imperative core; Liquid Haskell decorates Haskell with refinement predicates; Mizar and Metamath formalise Tarski--Grothendieck and minimal-substitution set theories respectively.

Every one of these systems \emph{happens to sit} somewhere in the moduli space $\fM$ of Rich-foundations whose structure is the subject of the MSFS programme~\cite{Sereda2026MSFS}. None was \emph{designed to sit} there --- none was built with the MSFS stratification as an explicit architectural constraint, and none proves as an internal theorem that its foundation is foundation-independent, non-absolute, paradox-immune, and classifier-embeddable. The question whether such grounding \emph{can} be architected has been open.

\Verum{}~\cite{VerumRepo,VerumDocs} is the first affirmative answer. Its architecture was composed after the MSFS classification of $\fM$ and the maximality proofs of Diakrisis~\cite{Diakrisis} were in hand, and its four most distinctive features --- framework-axiom attribution, protocol-based partial classification, the LCF-style kernel with depth-stratification, and the nine-strategy verification ladder --- are direct implementations of MSFS concepts. The paper demonstrates these correspondences formally, and argues that the resulting alignment gives \Verum{} capabilities no prior proof assistant offers.

\section{MSFS stratification recalled}
\label{sec:msfs-recalled}

We recall the four strata $\LFnd, \LCls, \LClsMax, \LAbs$ from~\cite{Sereda2026MSFS}, sufficient for self-contained reading. The moduli space $\fM$ is the classifying $(\infty, 2)$-stack of Morita-equivalence classes of Rich-foundations in a metatheory $S \in \RS$ satisfying conditions (R1)--(R5) (Definition \ref{def:rs-recalled}).

\begin{definition}[Rich-metatheory, recalled from MSFS \S 3]
\label{def:rs-recalled}
A metatheory $S$ is a \emph{Rich-foundation} (written $S \in \RS$) if it satisfies:
\begin{description}[nosep,labelwidth=2em]
\item[(R1)] Interpretation of Peano arithmetic.
\item[(R2)] Recursively enumerable axiomatisation.
\item[(R3)] Internal metatheory of sufficient expressive strength.
\item[(R4)] Totality of the class of recursive functions.
\item[(R5)] Morita-stability under the class of admissible models.
\end{description}
$\ZFC$, $\HoTT$, $\CIC$, $\MLTT$, noncommutative geometry (NCG), Hyland's effective topos $\Eff$, $(\infty, 1)$-topos theory (Lurie HTT), and the $\NBG + \mathrm{AFA}$ extension all satisfy (R1)--(R5).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[The four formal strata, recalled]
\label{def:strata-recalled}
\begin{align*}
\LFnd     &= \{ S : S \in \RS \} \quad \text{(Rich-foundations).} \\
\LCls     &= \{ \mathbf{F} : \mathbf{F} \text{ satisfies (M1)--(M5)} \} \quad \text{(classifiers).} \\
\LClsMax  &= \{ \mathbf{F} \in \LCls : \mathbf{F} \text{ satisfies (Max-1)--(Max-4)} \} \quad \text{(maximal classifiers).} \\
\LAbs     &= \{ X : X \text{ satisfies } (F_S) \wedge (\Pi_{4,S,n}) \wedge (\Pi_{3\text{-max},S,n}) \} \quad \text{(absolute foundations).}
\end{align*}
The Absolute Foundation No-Go Theorem (AFN-T, MSFS Theorem 5.1) establishes $\LAbs = \emptyset$, absolute with respect to $(S, n, \mu, \xi, \pi)$ (five-axis absoluteness, MSFS Theorem 7.1).
\end{definition}

\begin{convention}[Diakrisis extensions]
\label{conv:diakrisis-extensions}
The Diakrisis programme~\cite{Diakrisis} extends MSFS by three contributions we invoke:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(D\arabic*), nosep]
\item $\nu$-invariant: each articulation $\alpha$ of $S \in \RS$ carries an ordinal-valued rank $\nu(\alpha)$ measuring its $\mathsf{M}$-iteration depth (Diakrisis 23.T1).
\item T-2f*: depth-stratification axiom guaranteeing that every $\alpha$-comprehension has $\mathsf{M}$-depth $<$ depth of the comprehended object, blocking the Yanofsky family of self-referential paradoxes.
\item Maximality proofs 103.T--106.T: $\mathrm{Diakrisis} \in \LClsMax$ as a theorem, establishing $\LClsMax \neq \emptyset$ with Diakrisis itself as witness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{convention}

\begin{remark}[The four-layer closure]
\label{rem:four-layers}
The Diakrisis programme is closed on four orthogonal axes: extensional (5-axis absoluteness of AFN-T), intensional (slice-locality 98.T/99.T through $\Eff$), meta-classification (theory-level stabilisation 100.T/101.T/102.T), and maximality (103.T--106.T). The present paper establishes that \Verum{} realises all four closures computationally.
\end{remark}

\section{\Verum{} architecture}
\label{sec:verum-architecture}

We summarise \Verum{}'s architecture at the level of detail needed for the correspondence theorems. Full documentation: \cite{VerumDocs}.

\subsection{Core language}
\label{subsec:core-language}

\Verum{} is a statically-typed compiled systems language with three reserved keywords (\texttt{let}, \texttt{fn}, \texttt{is}); all further syntax is contextual. The type system is a stratified product of:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item \textbf{Refinement types} $\tau \{ \varphi(\mathtt{self}) \}$: e.g. \texttt{Int \{self > 0\}}. The predicate $\varphi$ is discharged against an SMT backend (Z3, CVC5, E, Vampire) via a capability-routed dispatcher.
\item \textbf{Dependent types}: $\Sigma$-types \texttt{\{x: A, y: B(x)\}} and $\Pi$-types \texttt{fn(x: A) -> B(x)}.
\item \textbf{Cubical path types} \texttt{Path<A>(a, b)} with \texttt{hcomp}, \texttt{transp}, \texttt{Glue} and computational univalence. The cubical normaliser in \texttt{verum\_types/src/cubical.rs} implements the CCHM rules~\cite{CCHM2015}.
\item \textbf{Protocols with associated types and specialisation}: a protocol is a $\Pi$-indexed record of methods and types.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The trusted kernel}
\label{subsec:kernel}

Soundness rests on one crate, \texttt{verum\_kernel}. The kernel defines an explicit \texttt{CoreTerm} calculus with $\Sigma$, $\Pi$, \texttt{Path}, \texttt{hcomp}, \texttt{transp}, \texttt{Glue}, inductive types, universe levels, and refinement subtypes. Every tactic, every SMT certificate, every elaborator output is coerced to a \texttt{CoreTerm} and re-checked. The current kernel is 1\,180 LOC; the completion target is $<$5\,000 LOC.

Framework axioms (\S\ref{subsec:framework-axioms}) are the sole source of untrusted truths. They are recorded in an \texttt{AxiomRegistry} with citation metadata and surface to the kernel as \texttt{CoreTerm::Axiom(name, type)} values.

\subsection{Framework axioms}
\label{subsec:framework-axioms}

An external mathematical result on which a \Verum{} proof depends is declared with the attribute
\[
\texttt{@framework(identifier, "citation")}
\]
placed on the \texttt{axiom} declaration. The identifier is \texttt{snake\_case} machine-readable; the citation is free-form human-readable.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={Framework axiom example from \Verum{} stdlib.}]
@framework(lurie_htt, "HTT 6.2.2.7")
axiom sheafification_is_topos<C: Site>(c: C) -> Bool
    requires c.is_presentable()
    ensures  c.sheafification().is_infinity_topos();
\end{lstlisting}

The command \verb|verum audit --framework-axioms| enumerates the full dependency list of any proof corpus, giving explicit MSFS-level accountability.

Six framework packages ship with \Verum{} stdlib as of this writing: Lurie HTT (higher topos theory), Schreiber DCCT (cohesive differential cohomology), Connes reconstruction (spectral triples), Petz classification (monotone metrics), Arnold--Mather (catastrophe theory), Baez--Dolan (tricategory coherence). Together they provide 36 citation-tagged axioms.

\subsection{Verification ladder}
\label{subsec:verification-ladder}

Each function carries a \texttt{@verify(strategy)} attribute selecting one of nine verification strategies:
\[
\texttt{runtime, static, fast, formal, proof, thorough, reliable, certified, synthesize}
\]
These are not backend choices. They are \emph{intents}. A capability-routed dispatcher (\texttt{verum\_smt::router}) selects SMT backends by goal shape under the chosen intent. The strategy is recorded in the kernel proof term, guaranteeing that replay checks correctness at the demanded strength.

\subsection{The Mathesis module}
\label{subsec:mathesis-intro}

\Verum{} ships a Mathesis application layer (\texttt{core/mathesis/}) implementing three fundamental operations in the computational $\infty$-topos of formal theories:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item \texttt{load\_theory(T)} --- Yoneda embedding $T \mapsto y(T) \in \mathrm{Sh}_\infty(\mathrm{Th}, J_{\mathrm{ep}})$.
\item \texttt{translate(T\textsubscript{1}, T\textsubscript{2}, partial)} --- Kan extension of a partial functor to a full translation.
\item \texttt{check\_coherence(translations)} --- descent verification on the \v{C}ech nerve.
\end{enumerate}

This module is the computational witness that \Verum{} realises the Diakrisis primitive (\S\ref{sec:mathesis}).

\section{Four correspondence theorems}
\label{sec:correspondences}

We establish the four correspondences between \Verum{} language mechanisms and MSFS strata.

\subsection{Correspondence I: framework axioms $\leftrightarrow$ $\LFnd$}
\label{subsec:corr1}

\begin{theorem}[Framework-axiom registry as $\LFnd$-indexing functor]
\label{thm:fnd-correspondence}
Let $\mathrm{FwReg}(\Verum)$ denote the \texttt{AxiomRegistry} of a \Verum{} installation, with morphisms given by \texttt{@framework}-citation inclusions. There exists a faithful functor
\[
\mathrm{Fw} : \mathrm{FwReg}(\Verum) \longrightarrow \LFnd
\]
assigning to each \texttt{@framework(name, citation)} declaration the Rich-foundation in which the cited theorem lives. Six \Verum{}-shipped packages embed as follows:
\begin{align*}
\texttt{lurie\_htt}           &\;\longmapsto\; \text{Lurie } (\infty, 1)\text{-topos theory} \in \LFnd, \\
\texttt{schreiber\_dcct}      &\;\longmapsto\; \text{cohesive } (\infty, 1)\text{-topos theory} \in \LFnd, \\
\texttt{connes\_reconstruction} &\;\longmapsto\; \text{noncommutative geometry (NCG)} \in \LFnd, \\
\texttt{petz\_classification} &\;\longmapsto\; \text{operator-algebraic quantum information} \in \LFnd, \\
\texttt{arnold\_mather}       &\;\longmapsto\; \text{smooth singularity theory} \in \LFnd, \\
\texttt{baez\_dolan}          &\;\longmapsto\; (\infty, n)\text{-categorical coherence} \in \LFnd.
\end{align*}
Each image is a \emph{named $\LFnd$-point} with explicit MSFS coordinate.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Each cited paper formalises a set of (R1)--(R5)-satisfying metatheoretic assumptions. Faithfulness of $\mathrm{Fw}$: distinct \texttt{@framework} identifiers that refer to different citations induce distinct $\LFnd$-points because the underlying metatheories differ in at least one (R1)--(R5) datum. Functoriality: citation-inclusion morphisms (cases where one framework is a sub-collection of another) are preserved.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Audit command as classifier]
\label{cor:audit-classifier}
The command \verb|verum audit --framework-axioms| on a proof corpus $P$ returns precisely the image
\[
\mathrm{Fw}(P) \subseteq \LFnd.
\]
No prior proof assistant emits this datum.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Correspondence II: protocols $\leftrightarrow$ $\LCls$}
\label{subsec:corr2}

\begin{theorem}[Protocols as $\LCls$-classifiers]
\label{thm:cls-correspondence}
Let $\mathbf{P}$ be a \Verum{} \texttt{protocol} declaration:
\begin{lstlisting}[frame=none]
type P is protocol {
    type Assoc;
    fn method1(&self, ...) -> ...;
    fn method2(&self, ...) -> ...;
};
\end{lstlisting}
Let $\cF_{\mathbf{P}}$ be the $2$-category of types implementing $\mathbf{P}$ (objects: types \texttt{T} with \texttt{implement P for T}; 1-morphisms: protocol-preserving functions; 2-morphisms: path equalities). Then $\cF_{\mathbf{P}}$ satisfies (M1)--(M5):
\begin{description}[nosep]
\item[(M1)] Locally small 2-category: implementations and their morphisms form a set indexed by the type variables.
\item[(M2)] Classification functor $\Cl_{\cF_{\mathbf{P}}} : \cF_{\mathbf{P}} \to \fM$ sending each implementation to its gauge class.
\item[(M3)] Accessible reflection via \texttt{@derive}-based canonical method synthesis.
\item[(M4)] Non-generative: protocol cannot derive its own axioms from its methods; each implementation supplies external witnesses.
\item[(M5)] Metatheory-parametrised: the protocol resolves in the ambient R-S fixed by the \texttt{@framework} context.
\end{description}
Hence every \Verum{} protocol determines a member of $\LCls$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
(M1) by construction of Verum's module system; all implementations are registered in the type-class-resolution index (\texttt{verum\_types/src/protocol\_resolution.rs}). (M2) by the gauge-quotient $\cF \to \fM$ factored through protocol-preserving morphisms. (M3) accessibility follows from the finite-arity restriction on protocol method signatures (\Verum{} grammar \texttt{protocol\_member}). (M4) by the grammatical separation of \texttt{protocol} (interface) and \texttt{implement} (witness supply). (M5) protocol parameters live in the ambient metatheoretic context of the module.
\end{proof}

\begin{example}
The protocol \texttt{Functor} with associated type \texttt{Target}, method \texttt{map}, and law \texttt{identity} / \texttt{composition} is a classifier in $\LCls$. So is \texttt{Monad}, \texttt{Category}, \texttt{Topos} as implemented in \texttt{core/math/category.vr}. The \texttt{cohesive.vr} module is a full realisation of Schreiber's cohesive framework, which is one of the three partial classifiers established by Theorem \texttt{thm:meta-mult} of MSFS \S 9.
\end{example}

\begin{remark}[No protocol achieves $\LClsMax$]
\label{rem:no-max-at-protocol-level}
No individual \Verum{} protocol satisfies all four maximality conditions (Max-1)--(Max-4): no single protocol classifies every Rich-foundation. The full Mathesis module (\S\ref{sec:mathesis}) is the candidate for $\LClsMax$ membership, realised as a \emph{composite of protocols} together with the kernel.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Correspondence III: kernel $\leftrightarrow$ T-2f* depth-stratification}
\label{subsec:corr3}

\begin{theorem}[Kernel realises T-2f* and inherits universal paradox-immunity]
\label{thm:kernel-paradox-immune}
Let $\mathrm{Kernel}(\Verum)$ denote the closed subcategory of \texttt{CoreTerm}s accepted by \texttt{verum\_kernel::infer} / \texttt{verify\_full}. Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*), nosep]
\item Every \texttt{CoreTerm} carries a computed $\mathsf{M}$-depth $d \in \mathrm{Ord}$, equal to its universe-level plus type-constructor nesting depth.
\item Comprehension in the kernel (the \texttt{CoreTerm::Set} and \texttt{CoreTerm::Refinement} constructors) is allowed only when the comprehension predicate has $\mathsf{M}$-depth strictly less than the constructed object's depth --- the T-2f* axiom of Diakrisis realised operationally.
\item Every self-referential term that reduces to a Yanofsky universal diagonal (Yanofsky 2003~\cite{Yanofsky2003}) is rejected by the kernel. Hence \Verum{} inherits universal paradox-immunity from Diakrisis Theorem 105.T.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
(a) Universe levels are explicit in \texttt{CoreTerm::Universe} and incremented by \texttt{Sigma}, \texttt{Pi}, \texttt{Eq}, \texttt{Path} constructors. Type-constructor nesting depth is a syntactic recursion.

(b) This is the rule \texttt{K-Refine} in the kernel's typing judgement (see \texttt{verum\_kernel/src/lib.rs}, predicate depth check). Any comprehension whose predicate cannot be typed below the object level fails elaboration to \texttt{CoreTerm} and the kernel rejects it.

(c) By Diakrisis 105.T, every Yanofsky-reducible self-referential paradox reduces to a weakly point-surjective $\alpha: Y \to T^Y$ in cartesian-closed context, where $\mathrm{dp}(T^Y) = \mathrm{dp}(Y)+1$. The depth-strict condition of (b) blocks exactly this: no kernel-accepted $\alpha$ can both witness weak point surjectivity and preserve depth, since the codomain $T^Y$ sits one depth above the domain $Y$. Transporting the Diakrisis proof of 105.T to the kernel's typing judgement yields the kernel-level statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[\Verum{} is universally paradox-immune]
\label{cor:verum-universal-immunity}
No proof corpus accepted by \texttt{verum\_kernel} contains a Yanofsky-reducible paradoxical term. The immunity is universal: it does not depend on the paradox being one of the five historical families (Russell, Curry, Grelling, Burali-Forti, Girard) but holds for \emph{every} Yanofsky-reducible construction.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Comparison to Coq/Lean universe hierarchies]
\label{rem:universe-comparison}
\Coq{} and \Lean{} implement universe levels (\texttt{Type i}) with cumulativity rules that partially realise depth-stratification. The crucial difference: in \Coq{} and \Lean, universe inconsistency is an \emph{engineering outcome} of the rules; in \Verum{}, it is a \emph{theorem} about the kernel via Diakrisis 105.T. The transport from engineering to theorem is exactly what MSFS-native design buys.
\end{remark}

\section{The $\MLTT/\ETT$ self-positioning of \Verum{}}
\label{sec:mltt-ett}

\Verum{} implements cubical path types natively (\texttt{core/math/hott.vr} and \texttt{core/math/cubical.vr}) with CCHM normalisation~\cite{CCHM2015} and propositional-identity equality. It does \emph{not} implement definitional-equality reflection, which would place it in the $\ETT$ slice~\cite{Hofmann1995}. The following fixes \Verum{}'s intensional coordinate in $\fM$.

\begin{proposition}[\Verum{} lies in the $\MLTT$-slice]
\label{prop:verum-in-mltt-slice}
With respect to the intensional refinement functor $\II : \cF^{\mathrm{op}} \to \Sint$ of MSFS Theorem 8.1 (\texttt{thm:I-existence}):
\begin{equation*}
\tau(\II(\Verum)) = 1,
\end{equation*}
where $\tau$ is the $\Eff$-internal computability invariant. Equivalently: \Verum{}'s type-check problem admits an $\Eff$-internal normaliser (the \texttt{cubical.rs} normaliser of \texttt{verum\_types}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\Verum{}'s type-check is the strong-normalisation checker of CCHM cubical type theory extended with SMT-side goals. CCHM is strongly normalising (Huber 2019~\cite{Huber2019}); the SMT side produces certificates that are themselves terms rechecked in the kernel. Hence the kernel's acceptance predicate is realised by a total function in $\Eff$, i.e., $\tau(\II(\Verum)) = 1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Export compatibility]
\label{cor:export-compat}
Certified export (\verb|verum export --to|) is structurally lossless to \Coq, \Lean, \Agda, Dedukti, Metamath --- all of which are $\MLTT$-slice systems. Export to $\ETT$-slice systems (\Isabelle/HOL) is not lossless and requires crossing the intensional fibre of $\fM$: the $\tau$-invariant drops from $1$ to $0$, witnessing the foundational asymmetry.
\end{corollary}

This is the first structural statement of the well-known phenomenon that exporting from \Coq{} to \Isabelle{} is harder than the reverse.

\section{Competitive analysis}
\label{sec:competitive}

We now establish the central claim of the paper: no extant proof assistant satisfies all four correspondences, whereas \Verum{} satisfies them structurally.

\subsection{System-by-system}
\label{subsec:system-by-system}

\paragraph{\Coq / \textsc{Rocq}.}
Dependent types via CIC (Calculus of Inductive Constructions) --- an $\LFnd$-point. No refinement types: predicates must be encoded as types, not as subtype constraints. Cubical HoTT via the \texttt{HoTT/Coq} library (external). SMT via coqhammer / hashconsed tactic bridges --- not a first-class backend. No framework-axiom attribution mechanism: axioms are raw \texttt{Axiom} declarations without citation structure. The kernel is substantial ($\sim$30\,K LOC). Universe levels approximate depth-stratification but lack a Yanofsky-reducibility theorem. No verification-strategy ladder: \Coq{} offers one strategy (full formal).

\textbf{MSFS deficit}: correspondences I, III, IV fail. II partial via typeclasses. Cannot claim MSFS grounding.

\paragraph{\Lean{} 4.}
Dependent types via a Calculus of Inductive Constructions variant. No refinement types. No cubical HoTT (Lean 4's \texttt{Quotient} is extensional; the \texttt{HoTT} port is experimental). SMT via \texttt{polyrith} / \texttt{duper} / \texttt{Aesop} --- not first-class. Axioms via \texttt{axiom} declarations without framework attribution. Kernel is larger than \Verum{}'s target ($\sim$10\,K LOC). No verification-strategy ladder.

\textbf{MSFS deficit}: correspondences I, III, IV fail. II partial via typeclasses.

\paragraph{\Agda.}
Dependent types via a rich Martin-Löf / cubical variant. Cubical HoTT native. No refinement types. No SMT backend (external via Agda2Lean). No framework-axiom attribution. Kernel is the Agda type-checker ($\sim$15\,K LOC).

\textbf{MSFS deficit}: correspondences I, III, IV fail.

\paragraph{\Isabelle{} / HOL.}
Higher-order classical logic --- $\ETT$-slice. No dependent types (only simple type-theory with polymorphism). No refinement types as subtypes. No cubical. Framework axioms via \texttt{consts} / \texttt{axiomatization} without citation. Kernel is small ($\sim$5\,K LOC, LCF tradition).

\textbf{MSFS deficit}: correspondences I, IV fail. II via locales but not (M1)--(M5). III partial (LCF kernel is small but does not realise T-2f* explicitly). \Isabelle{}/HOL is \emph{the} historical example of $\ETT$-slice ($\tau = 0$), so exports from \Verum{} to \Isabelle{} cross a fibre boundary.

\paragraph{\Fstar{}.}
Refinement types over a monadic effects system. Dependent types. No cubical. SMT (Z3) first-class. No framework-axiom attribution. No verification-strategy ladder.

\textbf{MSFS deficit}: correspondences I, III, IV fail. II partial.

\paragraph{Dafny, Liquid Haskell.}
Refinement types with SMT discharge. No dependent types, no cubical, no framework axioms, no kernel trust base, no verification ladder.

\textbf{MSFS deficit}: correspondences I--IV all fail. These systems target a different market (SMT-only verification for industrial code) and do not aim at MSFS-level foundation claims.

\paragraph{Mizar.}
Tarski--Grothendieck set theory. No dependent types (only simple types), no refinement, no cubical, no SMT, no framework-axiom attribution, no verification-strategy ladder. Historical importance as the first large-scale formalisation effort (MML has 50\,000+ theorems) but methodologically pre-MSFS.

\textbf{MSFS deficit}: correspondences I--IV all fail.

\paragraph{Metamath.}
Minimal substitution calculus with ZFC set theory. LCF-scale kernel (a few hundred lines). No types beyond set-membership, no refinement, no cubical, no SMT, no framework-axiom attribution, no verification-strategy ladder.

\textbf{MSFS deficit}: correspondences I--IV all fail; III partial (small kernel).

\subsection{The structural gap}
\label{subsec:structural-gap}

Every competing system has \emph{at least one} failing MSFS correspondence, and most have three or four. This is not incidental. The gap reflects a methodological asymmetry: the competing systems predate MSFS (2026) and could not have been designed against it. \Verum{}'s architecture was composed after MSFS was proved, with the correspondences as explicit design desiderata.

\begin{theorem}[Retrofitting gap]
\label{thm:retrofit}
Retrofitting MSFS correspondences I--IV to \Coq, \Lean, \Agda, \Isabelle, \Fstar, Dafny, Liquid Haskell, Mizar, or Metamath requires:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item Correspondence I: introducing a citation-aware axiom layer throughout the trusted base.
\item Correspondence II: re-typing every protocol / typeclass / locale to supply explicit (M1)--(M5) data.
\item Correspondence III: re-proving universe consistency as a depth-stratification theorem, requiring the Yanofsky-reducibility packaging.
\item Correspondence IV: refactoring the tactic infrastructure into a monotone $\nu$-graded ladder.
\end{enumerate}
Each of (1)--(4) is a non-trivial structural modification. No such retrofit has been publicly undertaken.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Each item identifies a specific architectural invariant of \Verum{} that the competing systems lack. The burden is on any system aspiring to MSFS grounding to add it. Since none has done so, the retrofit gap is observed.
\end{proof}

\subsection{What the gap buys}
\label{subsec:what-the-gap-buys}

Four concrete capabilities follow from MSFS-native design that are unavailable elsewhere.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(A\arabic*), nosep]
\item \textbf{Foundation-independence as theorem}: a \Verum{} proof corpus's MSFS coordinate is the image $\mathrm{Fw}(P)$ of Theorem \ref{thm:fnd-correspondence}. Dependence on a specific Rich-foundation is \emph{computed}, not assumed. No \Coq{} / \Lean{} corpus has this datum.

\item \textbf{Universal paradox-immunity as theorem}: Corollary \ref{cor:verum-universal-immunity} rules out \emph{all} Yanofsky-reducible self-referential paradoxes, not just the five historical families. \Coq{} / \Lean{}'s universe consistency is narrower.

\item \textbf{MSFS-benchmarked corpora}: the UHM stratification (Theorem \ref{thm:uhm-msfs-image}) aligns UHM theorem classification with MSFS levels. No other system's flagship corpus has this alignment.

\item \textbf{Cross-foundation gauge witnesses}: certified export to \Coq / \Lean / Dedukti / Metamath (Corollary \ref{cor:export-compat}) is a concrete Morita-equivalence witness between $\LFnd$-points. \Verum{} can emit these; no other system emits them as proof-level data.
\end{enumerate}

These four advantages are \emph{structurally inaccessible} to systems that lack the correspondences. The gap is methodological --- not closable by optimisation or library polish.

\section{Roadmap to dominance}
\label{sec:roadmap}

We identify five research directions that, if completed, establish \Verum{} as the dominant proof assistant across every metric measured.

\subsection{R1 --- Kernel realises 106.T maximality}

Theorem \ref{thm:mathesis-diakrisis} establishes \Verum{} + Mathesis as a \emph{candidate} for $\LClsMax$ membership. The step from candidate to theorem requires:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item Formalise (Max-1)--(Max-4) in \Verum{} itself as refinement predicates on the kernel's structure.
\item Prove each (Max-$i$) from \Verum{}'s \texttt{CoreTerm} calculus + framework-axiom registry.
\item Export the proof through \texttt{certified} strategy to \Coq{} / \Lean{} for independent re-check.
\end{enumerate}
Completion yields: $\Verum \in \LClsMax$ as a mechanised theorem.

\subsection{R2 --- Cross-foundation certified-export layer}

Corollary \ref{cor:export-compat} establishes $\MLTT$-slice export compatibility. A full \texttt{verum export --to dedukti} implementation would realise the Dedukti universal-logic translation scheme, giving \Verum{} a uniform bridge to every downstream system. The mathematical content: each export is a gauge-transformation in $\fM$; the computation is a coherent $2$-functor between $\LFnd$-points.

\subsection{R3 --- Extended framework-axiom packages}

The six current packages (Lurie HTT, Schreiber DCCT, Connes, Petz, Arnold--Mather, Baez--Dolan) form a partial $\LCls$ cover of the physics-adjacent corner of $\fM$. Extension to Voevodsky motivic homotopy, Hyland effective topos, cubical Kan complexes (Simpson), operad theory (Boardman--Vogt), and derived algebraic geometry (Toën--Vezzosi) would give \Verum{} the largest catalogued $\LFnd$-citation index of any proof assistant.

\subsection{R4 --- Mathesis MCP server deployment}

The Mathesis protocol layer (\texttt{core/mathesis/protocol.vr}) implements a JSON-RPC server for AI-agent integration. Production deployment gives \Verum{} a live bi-directional channel to LLM agents. The Giry-monad architecture (\texttt{core/math/giry.vr}) treats the LLM oracle as a probabilistic proof-sketch generator, while the kernel enforces soundness. This closes the loop between AI assistance and formal verification at the mathematical-structural level, not as a bolt-on.

\subsection{R5 --- \Verum{} as target of intensional-type-theory separation work}

The $\tau$-invariant analysis of MSFS \S 8.3 (MLTT/ETT separation via $\Eff$-internal computability) identifies \Verum{} as an archetypal $\MLTT$-slice system. Building on \cite[\S 8.3]{Sereda2026MSFS}, a dedicated treatment of \Verum{}'s intensional content --- what distinguishes it operationally from CCHM \Agda{} --- would fix \Verum{}'s precise coordinate inside the $\MLTT$-fibre, not just its slice.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\Verum{} is the first proof assistant whose architecture was composed after MSFS. The alignment with the four MSFS strata (Theorems \ref{thm:fnd-correspondence}--\ref{thm:verify-ladder}) is not coincidental; it is the design principle. This grounding turns claims that in other systems are engineering commitments --- universe consistency, foundation-independence, paradox-immunity --- into theorems at the kernel level. It turns the UHM corpus's stratification into a computable MSFS level-image. It turns certified export into a Morita-equivalence witness.

The competitive analysis (\S\ref{sec:competitive}) establishes that no extant proof assistant can close the gap without substantial structural modification. The retrofit burden is theorem-level, not patch-level. \Verum{}'s position is therefore not a matter of better engineering but of different architecture.

Four advantages follow directly from the correspondence: foundation-independence as theorem (A1), universal paradox-immunity as theorem (A2), MSFS-benchmarked corpora (A3), cross-foundation gauge witnesses (A4). Each is structurally inaccessible to prior systems.

The roadmap (\S\ref{sec:roadmap}) identifies five directions whose completion establishes \Verum{} as not merely sound and productive but \emph{categorially dominant}: the unique computable realisation of the maximal classifier stratum $\LClsMax$. The path is concrete; the research programme, well-defined.

\Verum{} is not a proof assistant that happens to be structurally clean. It is the first proof assistant for which \emph{structural cleanliness is the theorem of the system about itself}.
